Extra Question Answer

1. Explain the main features of a partnership.

The main features of a partnership are:

  • Agreement: Partnership is formed based on an agreement between two or more persons.
  • Number of partners: Minimum 2 and maximum 10 partners (20 in banking business).
  • Sharing of profits and losses: Partners share profits and losses in a pre-decided ratio.
  • Mutual agency: Every partner acts as an agent of the firm and can bind the firm by their acts.
  • Unlimited liability: Partners are personally liable for the firm’s debts without limit.
  • Legal status: Partnership firm is not a separate legal entity.
  • Voluntary association: Partners voluntarily come together to run the business.

2. What is a partnership deed? What are its main contents?

A partnership deed is a written agreement that lays down the terms and conditions agreed upon by the partners. It helps avoid disputes and clearly states the rights and duties of each partner.

Main contents of partnership deed:

  • Name and address of the firm and partners
  • Nature and place of business
  • Duration of partnership
  • Capital contribution of each partner
  • Profit sharing ratio
  • Interest on capital and drawings
  • Partner’s salary, commission, and allowance
  • Admission, retirement, and death of partners
  • Dissolution terms

3. What is goodwill? How is goodwill treated in partnership accounts during admission of a new partner?

Goodwill is the value of reputation, customer loyalty, and other intangible benefits of a firm which enable it to earn more profit than a normal business.

Treatment of goodwill on admission:

  • Goodwill is valued and credited to old partners in their profit sharing ratio.
  • The new partner pays goodwill amount to the old partners as compensation.
  • If no goodwill account is maintained, adjustments are made through partners’ capital accounts.

4. Define ‘Mutual Agency’. Explain its significance.

Mutual agency means every partner is an agent of the firm as well as the other partners. Each partner can bind the firm and other partners by their acts in the ordinary course of business.

Significance:

  • Helps in smooth functioning as each partner can act on behalf of the firm.
  • Allows partners to manage different aspects without consulting others constantly.
  • Increases the risk of unauthorized actions as one partner’s acts bind all.

5. What are capital account and current account in partnership?

  • Capital Account: Records the initial investment or permanent capital brought in by a partner. It usually remains fixed.
  • Current Account: Records ongoing transactions such as share of profit, drawings, salary, interest, etc.

6. What is ‘sacrificing ratio’? How is it calculated?

Sacrificing ratio is the ratio in which old partners give up their share of profit in favor of the new partner on admission.

Formula: Sacrificing Ratio = Old Ratio – New Ratio

7. How is interest on drawings treated in partnership accounts?

Treatment:

  • Credited to the Profit & Loss Account as income.
  • Debited to the respective partner’s current account.
  • It compensates the firm for early withdrawal of funds.

8. What is ‘retirement of a partner’? How is goodwill treated at retirement?

Retirement occurs when a partner leaves the firm voluntarily or due to agreement.

Goodwill Treatment:

  • The retiring partner’s share of goodwill is calculated.
  • Continuing partners compensate the retiring partner in gaining ratio.
  • The amount is adjusted through capital accounts.

9. State the duties of partners towards each other.

  • To act in good faith and honesty.
  • To disclose all material facts.
  • To avoid conflict of interest.
  • To be fair and share profits/losses as agreed.
  • Not to compete with the firm.
  • To manage the business diligently.

10. Explain the accounting treatment for partner’s salary.

  • Salary is debited to the Profit & Loss Account.
  • Credited to the partner’s current account.
  • Paid to active partners as agreed in the deed.
