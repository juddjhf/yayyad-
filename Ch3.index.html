Class 12 Accountancy – Chapter 3: Retirement & Death of a Partner

1. What is retirement of a partner?

Retirement is when a partner leaves the firm with the consent of other partners.

2. What is the difference between retirement and dissolution?

Retirement: one partner exits, firm continues. Dissolution: firm ceases to exist.

3. How is goodwill treated on retirement?

Outgoing partner’s share of goodwill is compensated by continuing partners in gaining ratio.

4. Define 'gaining ratio'.

Gaining ratio = new ratio – old ratio; shows gain of continuing partners from outgoing one.

5. What is ‘settlement of accounts of a retiring partner’?

It involves payment of capital, share of profit, goodwill, accumulated reserves, etc. to the outgoing partner.

6. How is accumulated profits adjusted on retirement?

Credited to retiring partner’s Capital/Current A/c in old ratio.

7. What entries are passed for revaluation on retirement?

  • Asset ↑: Asset A/c Dr. To Revaluation A/c
  • Liability ↓: Revaluation A/c Dr. To Liability A/c
  • Transfer to partners capital in old ratio: Revaluation A/c Dr/Cr. To Capital A/cs

8. What is the purpose of Revaluation Account at retirement?

To record changes in asset/liability values and distribute gains/losses before settlement.

9. How do continuing partners pay for goodwill?

They debit their capital/current accounts in gaining ratio and credit the retiring partner.

10. What if firm has a credit balance in goodwill account?

Distribute to remaining partners in old ratio before settlement.

11. Explain settlement via lump-sum payment.

Firm or partners pay full amount to retiring partner as one-time payment.

12. What is the retiring partner’s loan account?

If payment deferred, amount is shown as loan owed by firm to outgoing partner.

13. When is retirement treated as a reconstitution?

Whenever change in profit ratio, goodwill, or revaluation is involved.

14. What is the journal entry for goodwill adjustment?

[Continuing partners’ capital A/cs Dr. To Retiring Partner’s Capital A/c]

15. How are capital accounts adjusted on retirement?

Adjust for revaluation, goodwill, reserves, accumulated P&L, and outstanding drawings.

16. What disclosure is made in Balance Sheet post-retirement?

Show updated capitals, reserves, revaluation balances, and retiring partner’s loan if any.

17. How does death of a partner differ from retirement?

Death is compulsory exit; retirement is voluntary.

18. How is deceased partner’s share of goodwill treated?

Paid to deceased’s estate by continuing partners in gaining ratio.

19. What is executor’s account?

It represents the estate of the deceased partner in the books of the firm.

20. How is profit up to date of death calculated?

Estimate profits from last account to date of death, credited to deceased’s account.

21. What entries are passed for revaluation on death?

Same as retirement: record asset/liability changes and distribute gains/losses.

22. How are accumulated reserves treated on death?

Credited to executor’s A/c in old profit sharing ratio.

23. Explain treatment of outstanding drawings on death.

Debit executor’s account for drawings not yet effected.

24. How is executor’s account settled?

Paid via lump-sum or installments, or transferred to loan account.

25. What journal entry is passed when executor is paid in installments?

[Executor’s A/c Dr. To Cash/Bank A/c] for each payment.

26. How is the executor’s account closed?

When full settlement done, executor’s A/c balance becomes zero.

27. What if firm absorbs deceased partner’s goodwill premium?

Credit goodwill A/c and debit executor’s in gaining ratio.

28. How do you adjust firm’s reserves on death?

Debit Reserves A/c; credit executor’s A/c in old ratio.

29. What is the journal entry for revaluation gain?

[Revaluation A/c Dr. To Partners’ Capital A/cs]

30. How is profit share on death date calculated?

(Actual profit for period ÷ number of days) × days till death; credited to executor.

31. How are drawings of deceased partner settled?

Can be deducted from executor’s entitlement or paid separately.

32. What disclosures appear in final balance sheet after death?

Show remaining partners’ capital, executor’s loan or liability, and adjusted reserves.
