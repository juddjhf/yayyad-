Class 12 Accountancy – Chapter 2: Admission of a Partner

1. What happens to the profit sharing ratio on admission of a new partner?

When a new partner joins, the old ratio is adjusted to accommodate the new share. The remaining profits are shared by old partners in their new ratio.

2. Define ‘sacrificing ratio’ and state its purpose.

Sacrificing ratio is the ratio in which old partners sacrifice their profit shares for the new partner. Sacrificing Ratio = Old Ratio – New Ratio It indicates how goodwill is shared.

3. What is ‘goodwill’, and how is it adjusted when a partner is admitted?

Goodwill is the intangible value of reputation. On admission:
  • Old partners get credit in sacrificing ratio.
  • New partner brings goodwill.
  • If no goodwill account, adjust through capital accounts.

4. Explain the purpose of the Revaluation Account at the time of admission.

Revaluation Account records asset/lability value changes. Net gain/loss is transferred to old partners’ capitals in old ratio.

5. What is the ‘new profit sharing ratio’ and how is it calculated?

It includes the new partner. Old partners’ new shares = old share − sacrificed share; new ratio is agreed by all.

6. Describe the journal entry for a new partner bringing in capital and goodwill.

  • Cash A/c Dr. (capital + goodwill)
  •   To Partner’s Capital A/c (capital)
  •   To Partner’s Capital A/c (goodwill)

7. How are reserves and accumulated profits treated on admission?

They are credited to old partners’ capitals in old ratio unless agreed otherwise, giving them their share in past profits.

8. What entries are passed to adjust asset and liability revaluation?

  • For undervalued asset: Asset A/c Dr. To Revaluation A/c
  • For undervalued liability: Revaluation A/c Dr. To Liability A/c
  • Transfer gains/losses: Revaluation A/c Dr/Cr. To Partners’ Capital A/cs (old ratio)

9. Explain treatment of accumulated profits/reserves on admission.

Reserves/profits are debited and credited to old partners’ capital/current accounts in old ratio.

10. What changes are made to partners’ capital accounts on admission?

  • Increase by capital brought in
  • Credit for goodwill
  • Share in reserves and revaluation gains
  • Reduction if sacrificed share

11. What is the meaning of ‘premium for goodwill’?

Premium is the amount paid by the new partner as goodwill compensation, over and above actual business value.

12. How is the sacrificing ratio calculated when old partners share equally?

If old ratio is equal (e.g., 1:1) and new partner takes ⅓, each sacrifices 1/6. So ratio is 1/6:1/6.

13. Define ‘revaluation reserve’.

It is a placeholder in capital accounts for undistributed revaluation gains if not immediately transferred.

14. What journal entry is passed for revaluation loss?

Partners’ Capital A/cs Dr. To Revaluation A/c (in old ratio)

15. How is goodwill treated if no cash is brought?

Credit new partner’s capital for goodwill and debit old partners in the sacrificing ratio.

16. What treatment is given to fictitious assets on admission?

Write off remaining amount: P&L A/c Dr. To Fictitious Asset A/c

17. How do you treat undistributed profit on admission date?

It is shared by old partners in old ratio via capital/current accounts.

18. What is the treatment of partner’s goodwill brought in cash in firm’s books?

Goodwill is credited to old partners in sacrificing ratio via their capital accounts.

19. Give the entry for revaluation gain transferred to partners.

Revaluation A/c Dr. To Partners’ Capital A/cs (old ratio)

20. Explain the effect of admission on capital structure.

Capital structure changes: new partner’s capital + adjustments for goodwill, reserves, and revaluation.

21. How is new partner’s share of accumulated profit calculated?

New share × accumulated profit balance = their share brought in via capital/current account.

22. What is the ‘acquiring ratio’?

It’s the ratio in which old partners gain the retiring partner’s share, similarly used on admission sacrifice clarity.

23. How do you adjust bills receivable/payable on admission?

Revalue bills if undervalued – debit or credit Revaluation A/c, then share impact.

24. Why is revaluation necessary at admission?

To ensure fair value distribution and reflect true worth of assets/liabilities before new partner joins.

25. What happens when partner brings property instead of cash?

Partner’s capital credited and asset account debited at agreed value.

26. How to treat partner’s loan on admission?

Loan remains liability; interest is apportioned as per agreement.

27. What if new partner does not bring goodwill?

Adjust goodwill via capital accounts by debiting old partners’ and crediting new one.

28. What is the entry for partner bringing both capital and goodwill separately?

Cash A/c Dr. (capital)
Cash A/c Dr. (goodwill)
 To Partner’s Capital A/c (capital + goodwill)

29. What disclosures are shown in the Balance Sheet after admission?

Show new capital balance, goodwill (if retained), revaluation reserve, and updated reserves.

30. Why is clarity in profit ratios important on admission?

It ensures transparency, fair adjustment of goodwill, profits, and legal clarity in partner shares.
